# Opportunity intelligence and specialized studios

KnowledgeForge treats captured material as evidence, not merely text to summarize.
Its private profile combines owner-declared direction, an uploaded CV, skills,
interests, goals, location, preferences, and constraints. AI-suggested profile
changes remain separate until the owner explicitly approves them.

## Opportunity lifecycle

The discovery pass considers career, business, book, project, learning,
collaboration, speaking, grant, community, and nonprofit possibilities. Each
suggestion records fit, confidence, supporting note IDs, capability gaps, effort,
expected value, risks, and a small next step.

The lifecycle is:

`new -> saved or validated -> exploring -> completed`

An owner can also dismiss a suggestion. Refreshing discovery replaces only new
suggestions; saved, validated, accepted, and dismissed decisions are preserved.

Internet validation is approval-based. KnowledgeForge sends a narrow query made
from the opportunity title and profile location to a public search endpoint. It
does not send the CV or private library text to the search service. The selected
AI provider receives the opportunity summary and returned public snippets to
produce an evidence-qualified report with URLs and a retrieval date.

## Purpose-specific studios

All studios share sources, revisions, instructions, tasks, completion records,
and AI-provider controls. Their reference cards and presentation adapt to intent.

### Book Studio

- outline, parts, chapters, and scenes;
- scene cards with synopsis, conflict, point of view, setting, and chronology;
- character, location, theme, and research reference cards;
- continuity questions and unresolved canon;
- a living manuscript, revisions, writing direction, goals, and export.

The design takes inspiration from the linked outline/corkboard/editor model used
by long-form writing tools, while keeping KnowledgeForge's source-note evidence
and provider-neutral AI workflow.

### Venture Studio

- customer and problem;
- existing alternatives;
- value proposition and solution;
- channels, revenue, costs, and key metrics;
- assumptions, experiments, evidence, risks, and advantages.

The canvas is treated as a set of hypotheses. AI must label assumptions and
recommend validation before presenting them as facts.

### Impact Studio

- need and intended beneficiaries;
- inputs, activities, outputs, outcomes, and impact;
- indicators, baselines, targets, partners, and safeguards;
- assumptions, risks, unintended effects, and sustainability.

This follows a Theory-of-Change approach so an activity is not mistaken for an
outcome or impact.

## Completion

Finishing the last open task reveals **Complete workspace**. Completion always
requires confirmation. KnowledgeForge then preserves a snapshot, reviews
outcomes and deliverables, records lessons and unresolved work, suggests
follow-up opportunities, and places demonstrated-skill suggestions in the
profile approval queue. A completed workspace remains readable and can be
reopened.
