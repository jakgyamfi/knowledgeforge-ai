# Product-pattern research

KnowledgeForge borrows product patterns, not proprietary implementation or
copy, from established writing and execution systems.

## Patterns now implemented

- **Idea-to-outline structure:** Plottr centers chapters, plotlines, and scene
  cards in a visual timeline and can derive an outline from that structure.
  KnowledgeForge's book accelerator therefore produces ordered chapter briefs,
  scenes/evidence, themes, continuity questions, research gaps, and writing
  actions from captured source material.
- **Story bible and continuity:** Plottr connects scene cards to characters,
  places, tags, and a story/series bible. KnowledgeForge represents these as
  editable workspace-card collections so later source notes can update the
  evolving book context.
- **Context beside execution:** Notion places projects and tasks beside docs
  and knowledge, with timeline and dependency views. KnowledgeForge keeps
  source evidence, living documents, workspace structure, and estimated tasks
  in one workspace.
- **Outcome alignment:** Atlassian treats goals as outcomes to which multiple
  projects contribute and its planning tools expose dependencies and capacity.
  KnowledgeForge links workspace actions to the unified growth queue and asks
  the accelerator for success measures, milestones, risks, and ordered work.
- **Human approval:** Research and improved drafts remain proposals until the
  owner adds them. Accelerator runs are owner-triggered, and unsupported claims
  must remain hypotheses.

## Next candidates

1. Drag-and-drop chapter/scene board with explicit order.
2. Snapshot comparison and selective rollback for individual sections.
3. Character, setting, concept, and claim relationship graph.
4. Writing targets with word-count and revision-pass progress.
5. Calendar view and capacity-aware scheduling.
6. Explicit goal → outcome → milestone → task traceability.
7. Evidence freshness and contradiction alerts.
